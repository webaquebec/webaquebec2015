Wearables, Data, and the Changing Face of Identity

Ven 20 15h 10 Salle Bell · Keynotes et ateliers

Whether you’re lining up for an Apple Watch, using the heads-up display of Google Glass, or sporting one of the hundreds of activity and sleep trackers, it’s clear that wearable technology is hot.  We’re moving toward a world where all devices are becoming connected devices, and the market is exploding. 

However, the real promise of these wearables comes not from the hardware itself, but from the data and the information it represents.  Previously, our phones, cars, homes, and clothing were physical representations of aesthetic choices and our values.  As these devices become connected, they are increasingly becoming extensions of our digital selves as well.  In a world of ubiquitous data, our physical and digital selves have become inextricably intertwined.  How will wearables and the influx of new data shape our concepts of identity?

À propos
du Conférencier

Rachel Kalmar

Rachel Kalmar is a data scientist, community organizer, and world record holder for number of wearable sensors worn daily.  Wrangling noisy data, she investigates how to make wearable and sensor data useful and interactive.  A Stanford neuroscience PhD, Rachel has spent over a decade using data to explain, predict and influence behavior.  She is active in the Bay Area hardware community and runs Sensored, a 1000+ person meetup group for people working on sensor devices and applications (meetup.com/Sensored).  Combining her love of data, the brain, and novel visualizations, Rachel spends her weekends working on Dr. Brainlove, an interactive data platform that is a brain-shaped jungle gym the size of a bus.  Rachel is an alum of the d.school, Singularity University, Rock Health, and Misfit Wearables. Her favorite hashtag is #geekparadise.
